What is an Integrafintech High Yield Savings Account

An Integrafintech high yield savings account is a type of savings account that typically offers significantly higher rates on deposits when compared to the national average for traditional savings accounts. Higher interest rates mean your deposited funds grow faster, providing a greater return on deposits than standard savings account. In additiond to the benefit of better returns, an Integrafintech high yields account allows your money to effectively grow risk-free, making it ideal for storing short-term savings and specific savings goals. Another advantage for short-term savers: Unlike with a certificate of deposit (CD), you can generally access money whenever you want without penalty.

High Yield Savings vs. Stock Market

Investing in the stock market may bring historically higher annual returns. A High Yield Savings Account generally offers a competitive interest rates on all your deposits without the risk of losses. While the interest rate is variable and can fluctuate without notice, a High Yield savings account can be a great option for growing your savings while avoiding the risks associated with investing in the stock market.

FDIC Inurance

High Yield savings accounts are almost always insured by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC). FDIC insurance means that even if your bank goes out of business, your funds will be protected up to FDIC limits.

Compound Interest

Compound interest is a term for earnings on past interest earned. For example, if you earn
$10 in interest in January, February's interest calculation includes that $10 you just earned.
With compound interest your earnings can accelerate over time.

How to Calculate Interest on Integrafintech High Yield Savings Account

To find out how much you'll earn in interest through an Integrafintech high yield account, you can calculate it yourself using this formula: Interest = P x R x N
*P is the principal amount *R is the interest rate *N is the period (usually 1 is used to represent one year) Say, for example, you have an Integrafintech high yield savings account with a $5,000 balance (P) and an interest rate of 0.60% (R) over one year (N). Interest = $5,000 x 0.6% x 1, which equals $30 in interest.

Integrafintech High Yield Savings Account vs. Certificate of Deposit

When you put funds in a CD account, funds are locked into the account until a future maturity date in exchange for a potentially higher interest rate.
